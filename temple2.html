About The Temple

Sacred Presence in Kallahalli

The Sri Bhoo Varahanatha Swamy Temple is a 2,500-year-old mythological shrine. It is uniquely famous for its 14-foot tall monolithic Krishnashila idol of Lord Varaha, who sits with Goddess Bhoo Devi on his lap.

This "Shanta Murthy" form of the Lord attracts thousands of devotees seeking peace, prosperity, and blessings for land and housing. The temple is a prominent center of the Mysore Parakala Mutt.

Sthala Purana

The history of this Kshethra dates back to the era of Gautama Rishi, who performed penance here. Legend says the idol remained buried for centuries until it was rediscovered during the era of local chieftains. Today, the temple stands as a guardian of the Hemavathi river valley, representing the third avatar of Lord Vishnu.

Every corner of the temple reflects Hoysala and Vijayanagara influences, though the main sanctum retains its ancient, primitive power that devotees feel the moment they enter.
